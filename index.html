Our Story

Born from Flame

It started in a backyard in South Philly. Chef Marcus Rivera couldn't stop cooking over fire. Sommelier Elena Torres kept showing up with bottles that made the food sing. In 2019, they stopped pretending it was a hobby and opened a restaurant.

You'll smell the oak smoke before you see the kitchen. The hearth is the first thing you notice — a live fire that never goes out during service. Every cut is seared over cherrywood embers. Every vegetable is charred to the edge of caramelization. The farms are within 50 miles because Marcus picks up half the produce himself on Tuesday mornings.

The room is warm walnut and soft Edison light. There's vinyl on a turntable — usually jazz, sometimes Motown if the kitchen is in a mood. You can sit at the counter and watch the fire work, or settle into a corner booth and let the evening unfold. Either way, you'll leave planning your next visit.
